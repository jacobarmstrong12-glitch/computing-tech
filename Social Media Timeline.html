Talkomatic, 1973

Talkomatic is created by Dave Woolley and Douglas Brown at the University of Illinois, as a multi-user chat room application. It is an instant sensation among users in the PLATO System's online community.

Bulletin Board Systems, 1970s-1980s

Bulletin Board Systems (BBS) were computer systems running software that allowed users to connect via dial-up modems to access shared resources and communicate with other users. Some BBS progams even included games and file-sharing capabilities.

SixDegrees.com, 1997

SixDegrees.com was a social networking service website that was based on the Web of Contacts model of social networking. It was named after the concept of six degrees of separation and is often seen as the first social media platform.

Social media boom, 2000s

The most popular social media platforms were created, lots of which are still used today, these include 4chan, Myspace, Skype, Facebook, Youtube and tumbler

Vine, 2013

Vine was a social media platform that allowed users to create and share short video clips that were up to 6 seconds long, Vine was extremely popular among the younger generation and created the framework of modern social media sites.

Tiktok, 2017

Tiktok launched in 2017 which was soon after vine was discontinued, this helped tiktok gain popularity among users because it had a similar concept but with improved features. Tiktok is currently the most used social media platform based on hours spent.

Elon Musk buys twitter, 2022

Elon MUsk bought twitter for $44 billion, he renamed the platform to "X" and made significant changes to the platform including firing most employees. including the cheif executive and cheif financial officer.

Sora 2 launches, 2025

Sora 2, an AI video generation model is launched which causes users to create videos and clips with extremely little effort. This causes an AI revolution which changes the way we consume video content making it worse with AI becoming increasingly popular.

Australian social media ban, 2025

Anthony Albanese banned most of the social media platforms for under 16's in australia. This has influenced other countries to consider implimenting similar bans. The Australian ban seems to have made a insignificant diference as most teens have reported the ban not working or impacting them.
