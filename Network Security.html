Threats To Network Security

Types Of Malware And Viruses

Trojan Horse: A Torjan Horse is a type of malware that disguises itself as trusted or legitimate software in order to trick users into installing it. After the virus is installed, it can perform various malicious actions like stealing sensitive information, creating backdoors for hackers, or damaging the system.

Ransomware: Ransomware is a type of malware designed to lock users out of accesing their data until a certain amount is paid in order for the data to be released

Spyware: Spyware is a type of malware that is designed to gather information about a person or organization without their knowledge then sending that information to another entity to harm the user by violating their privacy

Keylogger: Keyloggers are not necessarily a type of malware, but they can be used for malicious purposes. A keylogger is a software or hardware device that records keystrokes made by a user. They can be used for legitimate purposes like monitoring employee activity, but they can also be used by hackers to steal sensitive information like passwords and credit card numbers.

Worm: A Worm is a type of malware that can self-replicate and spread across a network without the need for human interaction. Worms can cause significant damage by creating backdoors for hackers, stealing sensitive information, or consuming network resources.

Social Engineering:

Social engineering is a technique used by hackers to manipulate people into divulging sensitive information or performing actions that can compromise network security. This can include tactics like phishing, pretexting, baiting, and tailgating. Social engineering attacks often rely on psychological manipulation and can be difficult to defend against because they target human behavior rather than technical vulnerabilities.

Strategies To Prevent Cybercrime

Firewalls: Firewalls are a fundamental network security system that scans and monitors incoming and outgoing traffic between two networks based on defined security rules in order to filter out potential malware.
